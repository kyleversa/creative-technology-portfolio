# Get Lost

**Geolocation Game / Urban Experience Design / Site-Specific Interaction / Travel Discovery**

Get Lost is an interactive geolocation-based urban discovery system designed to help people explore a new city by intentionally getting lost in it.

The project combines digital and real-world components, using geolocation, wayfinding, storytelling, gamification, public space, and site-specific interactions to challenge traditional ideas of navigation. Rather than guiding users directly from point A to point B, Get Lost encourages discovery, curiosity, and playful exploration.

## Project Question

> How can an interactive urban environment system help people discover a city through navigation, disorientation, and play?

## Concept Overview

Get Lost explores the sensation of being lost as a design opportunity. The project investigates how people orient themselves in physical space, build cognitive maps, and discover meaning through movement.

The system uses geolocation zones, themed waypoints, public installations, street art, augmented reality markers, and hidden urban interactions to create a city-based experience that feels part game, part walking tour, and part discovery platform.

## Core Experience Elements

The proposed system includes:

- Geolocation-based exploration
- Real-world urban waypoints
- Digital and physical interaction layers
- Storytelling through place
- Gamified navigation
- Public installation art
- Street art and subculture motifs
- Augmented reality markers
- USB dead drops and hidden urban clues
- Commercial, creative, tourism, and entertainment applications

## Interaction Design

Users move through the city by following loose geolocation cues rather than direct instructions. As they enter different zones, they encounter possible waypoints that reveal stories, clues, art, or digital interactions.

The experience is designed to make users more aware of their surroundings and more open to unexpected discovery. The goal is not simply to arrive somewhere efficiently, but to build a richer relationship with the city through exploration.

## Early UI Exploration

Before developing Get Lost as a geolocation-based urban discovery system, I explored related travel interface ideas through an early concept called Allora. These mockups experimented with destination profiles, city pages, map-based discovery, and responsive mobile/desktop travel layouts.

While Allora focused more on digital travel content and interface presentation, Get Lost expanded the idea into a site-specific urban experience that combined geolocation, wayfinding, storytelling, public space, and real-world exploration.

## Reflection

Get Lost is especially relevant to my current work because it connects travel, interaction design, spatial experience, and product thinking. The project shows an early interest in designing systems that help people discover places through curiosity rather than efficiency alone.

It also connects conceptually to my later travel platform work by exploring how digital tools can make travel feel more personal, exploratory, and experience-driven.
