# The Unreliable Narrator

**Interactive Installation / Immersive Theatre / Spatial Experience Design**

The Unreliable Narrator is an interactive installation exploring memory, identity, persona, and unreliable storytelling through spatial design, found objects, theatrical triggers, and sensor-based interaction.

Designed and built as part of my thesis project for Concordia University’s Design graduate certificate program, the installation invites one participant at a time into an intimate, tactile environment filled with repurposed materials, lighting, sound, video, and embedded interaction.

Rather than watching a story unfold from a distance, the participant becomes an active reader and performer within the space. By examining drawers, objects, props, and fragments of a fictional character’s life, they uncover a non-linear narrative through physical interaction.

## Project Overview

The project began with a central design question:

> How can a physical space give the audience a more active and participatory role in a performative experience?

The installation was designed as a solitary, intimate environment where the participant could explore a fictional character’s memories through objects, textures, sounds, lighting changes, and theatrical cues. The goal was to shift the audience from passive spectator to active participant, allowing them to investigate the story by physically engaging with the environment.

## Interaction Design

The participant navigates the installation by exploring objects inside the space. Certain objects function as narrative triggers, activating changes in mood, sound, lighting, vibration, music, or other theatrical effects.

This creates a form of physical hypertext, where the story is not delivered in a fixed linear order. Instead, the participant discovers the narrative through movement, touch, attention, and curiosity.

## Materials and Build

The installation was built using mostly discarded, found, and repurposed materials, including wood, furniture, antiques, props, and embedded electronic components.

The project combined:

- Set and spatial design
- Found-object storytelling
- Fabrication
- Lighting and atmosphere
- Sound and video cues
- Sensor-based interaction
- Theatrical world-building
- Participant-led exploration

## Reflection

This project helped me understand how interaction design can extend beyond screens into physical environments. By combining theatre, installation, objects, and embedded triggers, The Unreliable Narrator explored how users can become active participants in a story rather than passive observers.

It remains one of my strongest examples of designing an experience that blends narrative, space, technology, and human participation.
